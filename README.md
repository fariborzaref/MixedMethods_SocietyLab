## Purpose

Before "mixed methods" was a term, it was a practice. Du Bois's *Philadelphia Negro* joined a door-to-door survey of five thousand households to ethnographic observation and historical analysis, not as decoration but as design: each kind of evidence was positioned to answer the question the others could not. The integration was the method.

Most research that calls itself mixed today is merely adjacent: a survey chapter and an interview chapter that never speak to each other. This repository is concerned with the harder problem, the architecture of integration itself. How should quantitative and qualitative components be sequenced, weighted, and merged? At what point does a statistical pattern hand off to interpretive inquiry, and on what warrant do the two yield a single conclusion? These are design questions, and design can be documented, taught, and replicated.

This is the fourth of four connected repositories, and the one that binds the others. [QuantitativeSocietyLab](https://github.com/fariborzaref/QuantitativeSocietyLab) holds the statistical models, [QualitativeSocietyLab](https://github.com/fariborzaref/QualitativeSocietyLab) the interpretive frameworks, and [ComputationalSociologyHub](https://github.com/fariborzaref/ComputationalSociologyHub) the methods of scale. This repository documents how their outputs are made to bear on one another. Materials are released in stages as the underlying studies conclude.

## The Design Program

**Convergent Designs.** Quantitative and qualitative strands collected in parallel and merged at interpretation. The documentation here addresses the merge itself: how divergence between strands is adjudicated rather than suppressed, since disagreement between methods is often the most informative result a study produces.

**Sequential Designs.** Explanatory sequences, in which statistical findings define what interpretation must explain, and exploratory sequences, in which fieldwork generates the constructs that measurement then tests. Templates here cover the connective tissue: sampling from one strand into the other, and translating findings across methodological languages.

**Joint Displays and Meta-Inference.** The integration of evidence must be visible, not asserted. Joint display templates place statistical and interpretive findings in a single analytic frame, and the meta-inference documentation specifies how conclusions drawn from combined evidence are warranted, qualified, and bounded.

**Case Selection at the Interface.** Which cases deserve close study is itself an inferential decision. Frameworks here use model results, residuals, deviant cases, and influential observations, to select sites for qualitative inquiry, so that fieldwork is targeted where the models are least adequate.

## Planned Architecture

Modules will be added as the corresponding studies conclude:

```
/Convergent_Designs     Parallel-strand design templates and divergence protocols
/Sequential_Designs     Explanatory and exploratory sequence documentation
/Joint_Displays         Templates for integrated presentation of mixed evidence
/Case_Selection         Model-guided frameworks for selecting qualitative cases
```

## Toolchain

`R` · `NVivo` · `LaTeX` · `Git`

Documentation is written so that the integration logic, the part of mixed-methods research least often reported and most often needed, can be reconstructed and reused by another researcher.
